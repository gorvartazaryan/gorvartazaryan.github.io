my (academic) journey

Highschool 😎

My academic journey began at Gymnázium Jana Keplera, which remains one of the most formative and enjoyable periods of my life. During my high-school years, I became interested in both the social sciences and humantities. I didn’t study much and spend most of my time with the legend - David Vávra. Unfortunately, he had to leave for our final year and I locked in… That year, I completed a SOČ (Student thesis), which placed 3rd in regional and 8th in the national competition. Together with friends, I co-founded Mladá Politika and organized many debates in Václav Havels’ library, recorded a podcast series with young assistants of MPs and even filmed a beerpong game with an opponents and supporters of adopting the euro in the Czech Republic. I also became involved in Fridays for Future Czech Republic and organized multiple demonstrations in Prague. This experience thought me a lot about politics, media and how public opinion shifts…

Chess ♟️

For many years, I was convinced that chess would become my primary intellectual pursuit. Unfortunately, my rating disagreed.

I spent most of my playing career oscillating between 1600 and 1900 Elo, representing ŠK Kobylysy and later Dukla Praha. The moment I realized that becoming a grandmaster was probably not in the cards came during the 2017 Czech Junior Championship Semifinals, when I managed to score only half a point in six rounds.

Despite this humbling experience, I never stopped playing. Chess taught me consistency, resilience, and—perhaps most importantly—how not to be a sore loser.

These days, I play mostly online on lichess.org, but I still occasionally make time for over-the-board tournaments. During my year at Oxford, I spent a great deal of time at the Oxford University Chess Club and even represented Linacre College in the annual Cuppers competition.

Then Everything Went Downhill ⛷️

At some point, I had to figure out what to do with my life, as becoming a chess grandmaster was clearly not going to happen. I therefore made the questionable decision to study law and political science at Charles University.

What initially seemed like a temporary detour gradually became a professional vocation. Along the way, I was fortunate to meet Prof. Jan Wintr (my current PhD supervisor), doc. Miloš Brunclík, and doc. Hana Kubátová. The latter two supervised my theses at the Faculty of Social Sciences, and all three played a major role in shaping my interest in academic research.

It was doc. Kubátová who first suggested that I should publish my bachelor’s thesis as a journal article. Over the following years, I began publishing regularly, secured three consecutive research grants from the Charles University Grant Agency (with an acceptance rate of roughly 30%), and gradually became more involved in academic life. More information about these projects can be found in the Research Projects section of this website.

After MANY sleepless nights spend in NTK (Czech National Library of Technology) I completed degrees in both disciplines and earned the sacred titles of JUDr. and PhDr. There are no direct equivalents to these qualifications in most other countries. Essentially, they are meaningless - just a basic equivalent of masters degree. However, in Czechia they retain remarkable cultural significance and people will happily address you as “Doctor” even though you do not hold a doctorate.

Academia 🎓

After completing both degrees, I naturally continued to a PhD at the Constitutional Law Department of Charles University. Although I was not initially selected for the department’s internal doctoral position—which provides PhD students with a monthly stipend—I decided to persevere and continue giving it my all.

After a year of hard work, I was offered the internal position by the department. At the same time, I was admitted to the University of Oxford to pursue an MPhil in Socio-Legal Research. I am currently combining both paths and am now in the second year of my doctoral studies.
