my (academic) journey

Highschool 😎

My academic journey began at Gymnázium Jana Keplera, which remains one of the most formative and enjoyable periods of my life. During my high-school years, I became interested in both the social sciences and humantities. I didn’t study much and spend most of my time with the legend - David Vávra. Unfortunately, he had to leave for our final year and I locked in… That year, I completed a SOČ (Student thesis), which placed 3rd in regional and 8th in the national competition. Together with friends, I co-founded Mladá Politika and organized many debates in Václav Havels’ library, recorded a podcast series with young assistants of MPs and even filmed a beerpong game with an opponents and supporters of adopting the euro in the Czech Republic. I also became involved in Fridays for Future Czech Republic and organized multiple demonstrations. This experience thought me a lot about politics, media and public opinion…

Chess ♟️

For many years, I was convinced that chess would be my primary intellectual pursuit. Unfortunately, my rating disagreed. I spent most of my playing career oscillating between 1600-1900 ELO, representing ŠK Kobylysy and later Dukla Praha. Although I never became a grandmaster, chess taught me consistency and not to be a sore loser. Sometim

Then everything went downhill ⛷️

At some point, I made the questionable decision to start studying law and political science at Charles University. What initially seemed like a temporary detour gradually became a professional vocation. Over the following years, I completed degrees in both disciplines and eventually earned the titles JUDr. and PhDr.

Academia 🎓

The decisive turning point came when I returned to the questions that had fascinated me since high school: institutions, democracy, constitutionalism, and the behaviour of political and judicial actors. This path ultimately led me to doctoral studies at Charles University and later to an MPhil in Socio-Legal Research at the University of Oxford. Looking back, academia was perhaps not a destination I planned from the beginning. Nevertheless, it became the place where my interests in law, politics, empirical research, and public affairs naturally converged.
