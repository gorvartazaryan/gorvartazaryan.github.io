## Highschool

![Gymnázium Jana Keplera](/assets/img/gjk.jpg){: style="width: 40%; float: right; margin-left: 20px;" }

My academic journey began at **Gymnázium Jana Keplera**, which remains one of the most formative and enjoyable periods of my life. During my high-school years, I became interested in both the social sciences and public affairs. I also completed a **SOČ (Student Professional Activities) thesis**, which placed **8th in the national competition**.

At the same time, I became increasingly engaged in civic and political life. Together with friends, I co-founded **Mladá Politika** and became involved in **Fridays for Future**, experiences that shaped my understanding of democratic participation and public engagement.

---

## Chess

For many years, I was convinced that chess would be my primary intellectual pursuit. Unfortunately, my rating disagreed.

I spent most of my playing career oscillating between **1600 and 1900 ELO**, representing **ŠK Kobylysy** and later **Dukla Praha**. Although I never became a grandmaster, chess taught me habits of analytical thinking, patience, and strategic decision-making that continue to influence my academic work.

![Chess](/assets/img/chess.jpg){: style="width: 35%; float: left; margin-right: 20px;" }

---

## Then everything went downhill ⛷️

At some point, I made the questionable decision to start studying **law** and **political science** at Charles University.

What initially seemed like a temporary detour gradually became a professional vocation. Over the following years, I completed degrees in both disciplines and eventually earned the titles **JUDr.** and **PhDr.**

---

## Back to academia 

The decisive turning point came when I returned to the questions that had fascinated me since high school: institutions, democracy, constitutionalism, and the behaviour of political and judicial actors.

This path ultimately led me to doctoral studies at **Charles University** and later to an **MPhil in Socio-Legal Research at the University of Oxford**.

Looking back, academia was perhaps not a destination I planned from the beginning. Nevertheless, it became the place where my interests in law, politics, empirical research, and public affairs naturally converged.
